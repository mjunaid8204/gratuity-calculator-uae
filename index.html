How UAE Gratuity Works

Understanding end-of-service benefits under UAE Labor Law 2026

1

Eligibility

Must complete at least 1 year of continuous service. Applies to both limited and unlimited contracts.

2

Calculation Basis

Based on basic salary only (excluding housing, transport, or other allowances).

3

Service Accrual

First 5 years: 21 days per year. After 5 years: 30 days per year.

4

Maximum Limit

Total gratuity cannot exceed 2 years of basic salary (cap applied automatically).

5

Payment Timeline

Employer must pay gratuity within 14 days after your employment ends.

6

Tax Treatment

Gratuity is generally tax-free for employees in UAE (no income tax on gratuity).

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common gratuity questions

What is gratuity and when am I eligible?

Gratuity (End of Service Benefit) is a payment employees receive when their employment ends. You're eligible after completing at least 1 year of continuous service, regardless of whether you resigned or were terminated by the employer (unless dismissed for gross misconduct).

Does gratuity include my allowances and bonuses?

No. Gratuity is calculated on your basic salary only. Housing allowance, transportation allowance, performance bonuses, and other variable allowances are NOT included in the gratuity calculation.

What's the difference between limited and unlimited contracts?

A limited contract has a specific end date and automatically terminates on that date. An unlimited contract continues indefinitely until either party terminates it. As of 2023, new contracts must be limited-term. The gratuity calculation formula is the same for both.

When does my employer need to pay gratuity?

According to Federal Decree-Law 33/2021, your employer must pay all end-of-service benefits (including gratuity) within 14 days after your employment ends. If payment is delayed, you can file a complaint with MOHRE.

Is my gratuity taxed?

No. In the UAE, gratuity received by employees is generally tax-free. There is no income tax deduction on end-of-service benefits. However, consult your tax advisor for specific circumstances.

What happens if I'm dismissed for gross misconduct?

Under Article 44 of the UAE Labor Law, if you're terminated for serious misconduct (theft, violence, breach of confidentiality, etc.), you forfeit your right to gratuity. The employer must provide evidence of gross misconduct.

How is gratuity calculated if I resign before 5 years?

For resignation before 5 years: you receive 21 days' basic salary for each year of service. However, the reason for resignation may affect your entitlement. If you resigned without a valid reason before 1 year, you get nothing. Between 1-3 years, you may receive reduced gratuity. From 3-5 years, you typically get the full 21 days per year.

Can I negotiate my gratuity amount?

No. Gratuity is mandated by UAE law and cannot be negotiated below the legal minimum. However, employers can offer more than the legal requirement. If you believe your gratuity is calculated incorrectly, file a complaint with MOHRE's Free Legal Consultation Service.
